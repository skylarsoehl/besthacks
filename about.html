Our Mission:

Our Story

BestHacks started in the late 50's when people started cutting their jeans into jorts. From stick-n-pokes to bleached hair we've been just a few steps behind the front line of every cultural zeitgeist (we did happen to miss those two trends, as we were trying to make a record player out of a corn husk and a lazy susan). We constantly strive to bring the public the best ways to utilize household items. We hope to provide people around the world with the resources needed to repurpose any object they have - bringing the dysfunction back to an increasingly functional world.
We firmly believe that any problem can be solved by a rubberband and a handful of expensive appliances. Instead of analyzing problems and identifying practical solutions, we choose to advertise solutions that involve as many disparate materials as possible. Many criticize our hacks for being impractical, time consuming, and "more work than they're worth".

Questions

What is even the point of these "lifehacks"? They seem pretty silly to me.

Why own one thing that does a task well, when you can own two things that can't do anything? That's really the motto here at BestHacks. We take what we have on hand or things that we find in the world and repurpose it. The time saved is negligible, and the utility gained is even less so - but the raw creative energy I feel walking into my bathroom and seeing my toothbrush, toothpaste, comb, and lotion that I made by destroying my kitchen pots and spending $100 on citrus and ginger at the market is almost palpable.

I spent 3 hours trying to make the soda can water kettle, but it takes over an hour to boil - did I do something wrong?

That is standard for the soda can water kettle. A couple suggestions for you - maybe use the wait time to try out a different hack, or learn a new skill. Or maybe just be appreciative of the fact that you can get rid of your old water kettle and embrace your inner Marie Kondo.

Following up on my previous question - I followed your advice and got rid of my old water kettle, but I still have to keep an old soda can around - how is this a benefit?
